There is a particular kind of resort that refuses to be ordinary, not because it tries too hard, but because its bones are simply too good to waste. The Diplomat Beach Resort has been that property since 1958, when the original hotel opened on Hollywood Beach and immediately became a magnet for South Florida's social and convention worlds. The towers have changed. The flags have rotated — Westin, then Curio Collection, now Signia by Hilton — but the pull of that wide Atlantic frontage has never required rebranding.

What has changed, meaningfully and visibly, is everything surrounding it.

### A $80 Million Reinvention, Built Around the Ocean

When Trinity Investments and Credit Suisse Asset Management acquired the property in February 2023 for $835 million, the question wasn't whether they would renovate — it was whether the renovation would be worth the noise. The answer, delivered across a phased program totalling approximately $80 million, is a confident yes.

The arrival experience sets the tone immediately. The reimagined porte cochère and front drive feel deliberate rather than grandiose, channelling what the design team describes as a *modern beach club spirit* — warm materials, open sightlines, and the faint scent of salt air even before you reach the lobby. The new lobby bar anchors the interior social life of the resort, functioning as the connective tissue between the check-in desk and the pool deck beyond. It is the kind of space that works equally well at 9am with a cortado and at 9pm with something colder.

Beyond the lobby, the renovation's most photogenic gesture is the new infinity pool, oriented directly toward the Atlantic. The geometry is straightforward — water meeting water — but executed with enough restraint that it earns its view rather than competing with it. Rebuilt cabanas line the beach club perimeter, and the overall poolside atmosphere reads closer to a private members' club than a convention hotel pool deck, which is exactly the tension Signia is trying to resolve across its portfolio.

### One Thousand Rooms and a 33rd-Floor Secret

With 1,000 guestrooms and suites across a high-rise oceanfront tower, the Diplomat operates at a scale that can feel anonymous in lesser hands. The Signia repositioning addresses this through a refreshed room palette — a contemporary, tropical sensibility that avoids both the beige neutrality of corporate hospitality and the overcorrected maximalism of some Florida competitors. The rooms are calm, well-lit, and oriented, wherever possible, toward that relentless blue horizon.

For guests who want a more curated experience within the larger resort, *Club Signia* on the 33rd floor is the answer. Approximately 6,660 square feet of combined indoor and terrace space sit at the top of the tower, offering priority services, exclusive access, and panoramic views that make the property's coastal positioning feel genuinely earned. It is the kind of floor that reminds you why elevation matters in a beachfront high-rise.

### Where the Resort and the Convention Center Shake Hands

The Diplomat has always straddled the line between leisure destination and major meetings venue, and the Signia conversion leans into that duality rather than apologising for it. The property now offers more than 200,000 square feet of flexible event space — four grand ballrooms, a 50,000-square-foot Great Hall with an open-air terrace, 39 breakout rooms, and beachfront terraces that make the Atlantic itself a backdrop for corporate gatherings.

This is, in fact, what Signia by Hilton exists to do. The brand occupies a specific and considered position within Hilton's portfolio: upper-upscale, meetings-centric, and large-format, sitting alongside properties like Signia by Hilton Orlando Bonnet Creek and Signia by Hilton Atlanta. The Diplomat is the brand's flagship beachfront expression, and the combination of genuine resort leisure and serious convention infrastructure is rarer than it should be in this segment.

For leisure travellers, the practical implication is that the service standards and logistical precision required to run a 200,000-square-foot event operation carry over meaningfully into the guest experience. The staff-to-guest ratio, the F&B execution, the room-readiness — these things benefit from the operational rigour that large-group business demands.

### Six Venues, One Through Line

Food and beverage at a 1,000-room resort is always a test of whether the property is serious about hospitality or merely going through the motions. At the Diplomat, the six primary dining venues hold together around a coastal, tropical identity that feels native to Hollywood Beach rather than imported from a design deck.

*Diplomat Prime*, the signature steakhouse, has been updated with new concepts and terraced outdoor dining that blur the line between interior restaurant and alfresco terrace. *Playa*, the oceanfront bar and grill, carries a Latin coastal influence that suits both the geography and the clientele — a menu built for people who have spent the afternoon in the sun and want something vivid and well-salted. *Point Royal* handles the coastal American register, while *Counter Point* serves as the morning coffee anchor. *The Hotel Bar* and *The Canteen* round out the daily-use venues, covering the grab-and-go and late-evening social needs that a resort of this size requires.

None of these venues is reaching for a Michelin star, nor should they be. They are doing something harder: feeding a thousand guests well, consistently, in an environment where the view is always the first thing anyone notices.

### The Hilton for Luxury Angle

For travellers who book through *Hilton for Luxury* — Hilton's dedicated program connecting affluent guests with the brand's highest-tier properties — the Diplomat's move to Signia is a notable development. Hilton Honors members engaging with the Hilton for Luxury tier will find the Diplomat positioned as a genuine resort asset rather than a transactional points redemption. The property's scale, its Club Signia access, and its beachfront infrastructure make it one of the more compelling Hilton Honors redemption opportunities on the East Coast for travellers who want both leisure depth and the logistical confidence of a large, professionally managed resort.

The Hilton for Luxury channel also typically unlocks a set of benefits — complimentary breakfast, space-available room upgrades, early check-in and late checkout, and a property credit — that, at a resort with a daily fee structure, carry genuine monetary weight. At the Diplomat, knowing those benefits are in play before you arrive changes the calculus of the stay.

### Hollywood Beach, Properly Considered

It is worth pausing on the location itself, because Hollywood Beach is perpetually undersold in comparison to its neighbours. Sitting roughly fifteen minutes from Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport, the resort sits on a stretch of Atlantic coastline that is broader and calmer than much of South Beach, without the density or the noise. The North Broadwalk is nearby for those who want to move through the neighbourhood on foot. Aventura Mall and Gulfstream Park Racing and Casino are within a short drive for those who want a change of scene.

But the honest version of a stay at the Diplomat is one where you leave the property as infrequently as possible — where the pool, the beach, the spa, the bar, and the evening terrace are sufficient, because they have been designed to be. That is the promise of the Signia repositioning, and on the evidence of what Trinity Investments and Hilton have produced together, it is a promise the resort is now positioned to keep.

### The Bottom Line

The Signia by Hilton Diplomat Beach Resort is not a quiet boutique discovery. It is a large, confident, beachfront resort that has been given a focused, well-funded reinvention and the operational backing of one of the world's most capable hotel companies. For travellers who want genuine ocean access, a resort infrastructure that does not compromise, and the loyalty currency of Hilton Honors working in their favour, the Diplomat belongs on the shortlist. Hollywood Beach has always had the bones. Now it has the hotel to match.

# The Emory London: A Masterclass in Quiet Modernity

There is a new architectural presence on the edge of Hyde Park. Standing adjacent to its sibling, The Berkeley, The Emory is the first new hotel in London from the Maybourne Hotel Group in thirty years. Designed by the late Lord Richard Rogers and Ivan Harbour of RSHP, the structure is immediately striking, featuring a glass facade suspended within a steel framework painted in a custom shade of "Emory Blue."

Inside, the hotel operates under a distinct design philosophy. Rather than utilizing a single interior designer, Maybourne commissioned a different world-renowned designer for each of the hotel's nine floors. Guests will encounter distinct aesthetic worlds designed by André Fu, Pierre-Yves Rochon, Champalimaud Design, Rigby & Rigby, and Patricia Urquiola, unified by a shared focus on residential scale, floor-to-ceiling glass, and curated art.

### The Design Philosophy

The result is a collection of suites that feel less like hotel rooms and more like exceptionally well-appointed private residences. Furnishings are bespoke, textiles are rich and textured, and the layout emphasizes space and natural light. It is a bold departure from traditional hotel design, offering a series of distinct spatial experiences under one roof.

For culinary offerings, The Emory welcomes Jean-Georges Vongerichten's ABC Kitchen, serving fresh, organic, and locally-sourced dishes in an airy, glass-walled space. The hotel also features a private rooftop bar, Bar 22, reserved exclusively for hotel guests and members of the private club.

### Exclusive Amenities and Wellness

Beneath the surface lies Surrenne, a subterranean wellness club spanning four floors. Offering a 22-meter pool, state-of-the-art fitness spaces, and bespoke wellness treatments, it provides a quiet sanctuary away from the bustle of Knightsbridge.

Through its combination of architecture, design, and personalized service, The Emory is poised to redefine modern luxury in London. It represents a shift away from traditional formality towards a more relaxed, private, and highly customized guest experience.

*Where the desert meets the water's edge, Hyatt's Miraval brings its most considered vision of stillness yet to the shores of Shura Island.*

## A New Chapter for Intentional Travel

There are openings that expand a brand's footprint, and then there are openings that expand the very idea of what a place can mean. Miraval The Red Sea — the newest address from Hyatt's celebrated Miraval Resorts & Spas collection — belongs firmly to the latter category. Settled on Shura Island along Saudi Arabia's Red Sea coast, this is not merely a resort that has arrived in a new geography. It is a philosophy — one built over decades in the desert landscapes of Arizona and the rolling hills of Massachusetts — that has found, at last, an entirely new elemental language to speak. The Red Sea, with its luminous shallows, its coral-fringed silence, and its ancient trade-wind light, offers Miraval something it has never quite had before: the sensation of being held between two vast, breathing worlds.

## The Landscape as Architecture

Shura Island sits within the broader NEOM development corridor, a coastline that carries the particular quality of light found only at the meeting of arid land and equatorial sea — golden, diffuse, and deeply unhurried. Miraval has always understood that landscape is not backdrop; it is architecture. The resort's design sensibility, as has come to define the brand across its properties, is rooted in the material grammar of its surroundings — raw stone, warm earth tones, textured plaster, and the kind of considered minimalism that does not announce itself but rather recedes, allowing the natural world to fill the frame. Here, terraces dissolve toward the water, indoor and outdoor boundaries soften in the salt-warm air, and the built environment seems less constructed than excavated from the land itself. Every surface, every threshold, every corridor has been calibrated to slow the body before the mind has even had time to agree.

## Wellness as a Lived Practice

What has always distinguished Miraval from the broader luxury wellness landscape is its commitment to depth over spectacle. The spa and wellness programming at Miraval The Red Sea continues this tradition with a rigor that is rare. Rather than offering wellness as a menu of treatments to be sampled at leisure, Miraval structures its approach around intention — guests are invited into a framework of daily rhythm, mindful movement, therapeutic bodywork, and contemplative practice that accumulates, over the course of a stay, into something genuinely transformative. The Red Sea setting adds dimensions that earlier Miraval properties could only approximate: water therapy takes on new resonance when the sea itself is steps away, and sunrise meditation carries an entirely different weight when conducted against a horizon that has been sacred to travelers and traders for millennia. The spa facilities themselves — drawn from Miraval's deep institutional knowledge — are expected to be among the most comprehensive in the region, with treatment rooms, thermal circuits, and movement studios that honor both the brand's American roots and the wellness traditions of the wider Arab world.

## An Address for the Genuinely Curious

Saudi Arabia's emergence as a serious luxury travel destination has been swift, deliberate, and, for those paying attention, entirely credible. The country's extraordinary natural assets — the Red Sea's biodiversity, the vast silence of its interior deserts, the layered history of its ancient cities — have long existed; what has changed is the infrastructure and international hospitality investment required to make them accessible to a discerning global traveler. Miraval's arrival on Shura Island is among the most significant signals yet that this coastline is not simply open for business but open for the kind of slow, considered, deeply personal travel that the luxury market increasingly prizes above all else. This is a destination for guests who arrive with questions rather than itineraries — who understand that the most valuable currency a resort can offer is not amenity count, but quality of attention.

## The Details That Distinguish

The accommodation offering at Miraval The Red Sea extends across a range of rooms, suites, and private residences — each conceived to feel less like a hotel room and more like a personal sanctuary calibrated to the rhythms of rest and renewal. Linens are weighty and cool; light enters rooms at angles chosen with care; the absence of televisions in certain categories is not an oversight but a statement. Dining, as at all Miraval properties, is understood as part of the wellness continuum — menus are built around nourishment and pleasure in equal measure, drawing on the extraordinary produce of the region while reflecting Miraval's long-standing culinary philosophy. For a brand that has spent decades refining what it means to care for a guest at the most fundamental level, the Red Sea property feels not like an expansion but like an arrival — the right place, at precisely the right moment.

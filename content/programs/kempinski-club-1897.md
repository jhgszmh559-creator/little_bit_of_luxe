# The quiet *permanence* of Kempinski hospitality

## An editorial reflection on Kempinski Club 1897, where travel is stripped of pretense and returned to its architectural and cultural roots.

There is a particular kind of hotel that does not need to announce itself. Its confidence is structural — present in the weight of a door handle, the proportion of a corridor, the way afternoon light falls across a marble floor that has been walked upon for generations. Kempinski Hotels and Resorts belongs to this category. Founded in 1897, it holds the distinction of being Europe's oldest luxury hotel group, and that lineage is not merely a marketing footnote — it is woven into the operational philosophy of every property in the portfolio. From a grand dame on the shores of Lake Geneva to a contemporary tower above the Dubai skyline, each Kempinski carries the same underlying conviction: that hospitality, at its finest, is an act of sustained attention. Club 1897 is the preferred partner program through which discerning travellers access that attention in its most tangible form.

What distinguishes Club 1897 from the broader landscape of hotel loyalty programs is its refusal to feel transactional. There are no tiers to climb, no points to anxiously accumulate before they expire quietly in a digital account you have forgotten the password to. Instead, the program operates on a more direct and honest premise — that a traveller booking through a recognised preferred partner channel should arrive already known, already valued, and already extended the kind of welcome that most programs reserve for their most frequent guests. In practice, this means complimentary daily breakfast for two, a priority room upgrade subject to availability, early check-in and late check-out where the house allows, and a property credit — most commonly one hundred US dollars — to be spent as the guest sees fit, whether on a long lunch, a treatment at the spa, or simply a bottle of something cold and well-chosen from the minibar. At select properties the credit sits at fifty dollars, a distinction worth noting when planning a stay, though the remaining benefits hold consistently across the portfolio.

The Kempinski estate spans more than eighty properties across five continents, and the range is genuinely broad — a restored palace in Prague, a beachfront retreat in Muscat, a mountain sanctuary in the Swiss Alps, a quietly spectacular address in Nairobi. What unites them is a commitment to local character expressed through a European sensibility: the interiors tend toward the considered rather than the theatrical, the service toward the warm rather than the performative. Staying within the Club 1897 framework does not alter the nature of these places — it simply ensures that your relationship with them begins on better terms. The room you are shown to is more likely to be the better room. The morning is more likely to begin without the small friction of deciding whether breakfast is worth the price. These are not dramatic gestures, but they are the kind of details that quietly determine whether a stay feels like an expense or an experience.

We have long believed that the most intelligent way to travel is not to spend more, but to spend with more intention — to choose programs and partners that align the economics of a booking with the quality of the stay, without asking you to sacrifice flexibility or accumulate obligations. Club 1897 sits comfortably within that philosophy. Rates booked through our preferred partnership are matched to the best available flexible rates offered directly by the hotel, which means your loyalty nights and points — if you hold a Kempinski loyalty account — are fully recognised and credited. Nothing is forfeited. The perks are additive, not conditional. For a group with this depth of history and this breadth of geography, that is a meaningful arrangement — and one we are glad to extend to our readers.
